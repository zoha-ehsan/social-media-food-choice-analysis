# social-media-food-choice-analysis
Quantitative research project analyzing the influence of social media on food choices among university students using logistic regression (IBM SPSS).

**Influence of Social Media on Food Choices Among University Students in Lahore**

**Overview**
This repository contains the dataset output, statistical analysis, and final report for a quantitative research project examining how social media exposure influences food-related decision-making among university students at Government College University Lahore.

**Research Objective**
To investigate whether social media usage patterns predict the likelihood of trying food items seen online.

**Methodology**
Cross-sectional survey design
Primary data collection (structured questionnaire)
Pearson Chi-Square tests
Binary Logistic Regression
Hosmer–Lemeshow Goodness-of-Fit Test
Correlation & Visualization (Python)
Analysis conducted in IBM SPSS Statistics

**Key Findings**
71.7% of respondents tried food solely due to social media exposure
Heavy social media users were significantly more likely to try food seen online
Gender showed a meaningful behavioral difference
Logistic regression model significant (χ² = 14.776, p = .005)
Model explained 31.3% of variance (Nagelkerke R² = .313)
Strong model fit (Hosmer–Lemeshow p = .954)
Overall classification accuracy: 80%
